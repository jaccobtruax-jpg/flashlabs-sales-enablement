"Totally fair. But inbound speed-to-lead is where AI shines. When a lead comes in, your AI agent qualifies them in seconds instead of hours. We've seen shops convert 30% more inbound by reducing response time from 4 hours to under a minute."

"When does it end? Let's put a calendar hold for the month before. I'll check in 60 days before — same deal, same terms, no pressure."

"Our customers don't share their secret sauce — and honestly, you wouldn't want them to. That's why the POC exists. 90 days, exit anytime, prove it to yourself."

🔒 POC & Closing Framework

Three Paths to Close

Most Common Path A: POC
"90 days, monthly payments, exit anytime. By week 4 you will be at or above your current numbers. If not, we part ways. No risk."

When: They need proof, coming from legacy system, 3+ seats, skeptical.

When Sold Path B: Direct Close
"You know the problem. You have seen the solution. The sooner you switch, the sooner your numbers improve. One quarter commitment."

When: Already sold, referral, or urgent pain.

Path C: FlashAI Standalone
"$1.50 per connected call, 500 call minimum. No seats, no per-user cost. Your reps focus on closing, AI handles opens."

When: Labor-constrained, high volume, want to test before committing.

POC Hard Rules

  • 90-day terms, monthly payments — no annual lock-in during POC
  • Exit anytime — builds trust, reduces risk perception
  • Never refund or discount during POC — hold the line on pricing
  • Concede on features/onboarding support, not price
  • Get them live FAST — speed kills doubt. Target 2 weeks to first calls

🔴 The Patterns: What's Going Wrong

Pattern 1: The 42% Ghost Rate

28 of 66 calls = answered but zero response. Prospect picks up, hears Danny's intro, says nothing or hangs up instantly.

Root Cause Hypothesis

🛡️ Don't Get Your Account Nuked

Hard Rules

  • Max 10 connection requests per day. LinkedIn's safety ceiling. Push past this consistently and you'll get restricted. No exceptions.
  • Max 20-50 messages per day. If you're sending identical messages at identical intervals, LinkedIn detects the pattern and flags you as automation.
  • Never use automation tools like Dux-Soup, WeConnect, Expandi, or any LinkedIn bot. LinkedIn's detection has gotten significantly better. A restricted account means zero outreach for weeks.
  • Never pitch in a connection request. This is the fastest way to get reported as spam. Connect first. Talk later.
  • Vary your messages. If every DM starts with "Hey {first_name}" and follows the same structure, you'll get caught. Write like a human who messages different people differently.
  • Don't name competitors by name in public posts. "We help teams upgrade from legacy dialers" — professional. "Vici sucks" — gets you in trouble and makes you look small.

What Keeps You Safe

  • Engage like a real person. Vary your activity. Some days you comment more, some days you DM more. Don't follow a scripted pattern.
  • Profile views are normal. Viewing 20+ profiles a day is fine if you're actually doing research. The alarm bells ring when it's automated.
  • Track everything in CRM. Every connection, DM, response, meeting booked. If LinkedIn ever questions your activity, you have a paper trail showing legitimate business development.
